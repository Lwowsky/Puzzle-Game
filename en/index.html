The Hidden Leaf Village welcomes you, Shinobi!

Here, puzzles aren’t “just pictures.” They’re chakra training for your attention. Every piece has its place, like a hand seal in jutsu. A precise match is a step toward your rank; a mistake is a lesson in discipline.

Here, chaos is only an illusion for those who rush. Turn on your “Sharingan” of focus: lines, shadows, and tiny details reveal the right path. Assemble the image—and with it, assemble control: piece by piece, like a shinobi in training.

How to start the game

  1. Choose your path through the ranks in the top menu (Apprentice → Hokage) or click Start mission/Continue.
  2. Open a chapter and launch the puzzle. In the game window, press “Start”—the board will shuffle.
  3. Choose a difficulty: 3×3 / 4×4 / 5×5. The harder it is, the more XP you earn.
  4. If you need a moment to focus, press Pause. Come back and continue—no stress.

What matters here isn’t speed, but your eye. Turn on your “Sharingan” of focus: notice small matches of lines and shadows. One accurate piece—one step toward your rank.

Shinobi techniques

  • Frame technique: start with corners and edges—your foundation.
  • Zone technique: split the picture into sectors and work one at a time.
  • Shadow technique: look for light/shadow transitions—they guide you.
  • Pause technique: if you’re stuck, take a breath. Your brain will rebuild the pattern.
  • Pattern technique: look for repeats (clouds, textures, hair, lines)—they connect pieces faster than guessing.

Progress and ranks

Missions give you XP and raise your level. A rank isn’t just a number— it’s proof you learned to see patterns where others see chaos.

Chaos always shouts. The right path is silent. Feel the difference—and the puzzle will flow.

Ready Shinobi? Steady your breath. Gather your will—and go piece by piece.
